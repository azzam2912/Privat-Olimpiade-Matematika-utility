    \item Determine the subsequent term in the following sequence:
    \[ 41, 32, 43, 74, 26, 97, 89, \dots \]

    % sasmo 2025 - question 18
    \item Define an operation $\bullet$ on pairs of numbers that yields the results below:
    \begin{align*}
        5 \bullet 3 &= 2458 \\
        8 \bullet 3 &= 822 \\
        15 \bullet 4 &= 1538 \\
        11 \bullet 8 &= 6338
    \end{align*}
    Evaluate $7 \bullet 2$.

    % sasmo 2025 - question 19
    \item Find the maximum four-digit integer that possesses exactly four distinct positive divisors.

    % sasmo 2025 - question 21
    \item Dividing 2028 by 85 yields a quotient of 30 and a remainder of 30. For how many positive integer divisors will the division of 2028 result in an identical quotient and remainder?

    % sasmo 2025 - question 23
    \item Suppose $\frac{x}{5}$ and $\frac{y}{10}$ are irreducible proper fractions. If their sum $\frac{x}{5} + \frac{y}{10}$ lies strictly between 1.51 and 1.52, compute $x+y$.

    % sasmo 2025 - question 24
    \item Let $ABCD$ be a square as shown, with points $E$ on $AB$ and $F$ on $BC$ satisfying $4AE = 3EB$ and $4FC = 3BF$. Given the side length $AB = 20\text{ cm}$, calculate the shaded area in $\text{cm}^2$.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 2
    \item Determine the number of trailing zeros in the decimal representation of $7^8 \times 10^4 \times 8^3 \times 25^6$.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 3
    \item A grid is formed by 144 unit squares. Removing two squares per row and adding four squares per column results in a new grid that also contains exactly 144 squares. Find the initial number of columns.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 4
    \item The provided illustration consists of a regular hexagon, a square, and a circle centered at $O$ with an area of $48\text{ cm}^2$.     \item In a particular month, exactly three Wednesdays occur on even-numbered days. What day of the week is the 25th of that month?

    % sasmo 2022 - question 12
    \item Define a \textit{beprisque} number as an integer adjacent to both a perfect square and a prime number. For instance, 8 fits this definition. Count the total number of three-digit \textit{beprisque} numbers.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 15
    \item Points $E$ and $F$ lie on sides $AD$ and $CD$ of a square $ABCD$ such that $4DE = 4FC = AB$. The lines $BF$ and $CE$ meet at $G$. Given that the square's area is $240\text{ cm}^2$, compute the area of quadrilateral $EDFG$ in $\text{cm}^2$.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 24
    \item Suppose $a, b, c, d,$ and $e$ are real numbers satisfying the following system:
    \begin{align*}
        a &= |b| - |b-1| \\
        b &= |c| - |c-1| \\
        c &= |d| - |d-1| \\
        d &= |e| - |e-1|
    \end{align*}
    Given $a = \frac{1}{3}$ and $e = \frac{x}{y}$ for coprime integers $x$ and $y$, evaluate $x+y$.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 25
    \item Let $A = \frac{2}{1+2^2+2^4} + \frac{3}{1+3^2+3^4} + \frac{4}{1+4^2+4^4} + \dots + \frac{20}{1+20^2+20^4}$. Using $[x]$ to denote the greatest integer less than or equal to $x$, compute the value of $[1/A]$.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 7
    \item Identify the number that follows in the given pattern:
    \[ 2, 9, 28, 65, 126, \dots \]

    % sasmo 2022 - question 10
    \item The number $A74A2$ is divisible by 18. Assuming the digit $A$ is not prime, determine the value of $A$.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 13
    \item Find the total count of five-digit positive integers whose digits sum to exactly 40.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 14
    \item Determine the number of distinct ways to write 85 as a sum of at least two consecutive positive integers.

    % sasmo 2025 - question 14
    \item Evelyn rides a bicycle from her house to the park at $30\text{ km/h}$, leaving at 8:00 a.m. Daniel drives in the opposite direction, from the park to Evelyn's house, starting at 9:30 a.m. at $45\text{ km/h}$. The moment they pass each other, Desmond begins cycling from the park towards Evelyn's house at $20\text{ km/h}$. Given that Desmond and Evelyn cross paths at the exact time Daniel arrives at Evelyn's house, calculate the distance between the house and the park in kilometers.

    % sasmo 2025 - question 16
    \item Evaluate the sum of the digits for the product below:
    \[ 4008 \times \underbrace{111\dots1}_{150 \text{ times}} \]

    % sasmo 2025 - question 17
    \item A train moving at $120\text{ km/h}$ takes 45 seconds to completely cross a bridge, and 30 seconds to pass a stationary man on that bridge. Find the bridge's length in meters.

    % sasmo 2025 - question 18
    \item Compute the sum of the digits of the integer $A$, defined as:
    \[ A = 6,666,666^2 - 3,333,333^2 \]

    % sasmo 2025 - question 19
    \item Consider an operation $\triangle$ governed by the following rules:
    $5 \triangle 2 = 4 + 5 + 6$
    $9 \triangle 6 = 8 + 9 + 10 + 11 + 12 + 13$
    Given $164 \triangle x = 2022$, solve for $x$.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 20
    \item A particular machine translates positive integers into two-digit codes using these rules:
    1. The first digit represents the remainder when the integer is divided by 7.
    2. The second digit represents the remainder upon division by 11.
    3. Should the modulo 11 remainder exceed 8, the second digit defaults to 8.
    For instance, the integer 15 maps to the code 14. Identify the smallest integer that generates the code 13.

    % sasmo 2022 - question 21
    \item Sisters Catherine, Stacy, and Fiona made the following claims about their ages:
    Catherine: "I am 17 years old. I am 4 years younger than Stacy. I am 3 years older than Fiona."
    Stacy: "I am not the youngest sister. The age gap between Fiona and me is 6 years. Fiona is 22 years old."
    Fiona: "I am younger than Catherine. Catherine is 19. Stacy is 4 years younger than Catherine."
    Given that exactly two sentences in each person's claim are true, determine Fiona's age.

    % sasmo 2025 - question 25
    \item Let $ABC$ be a right triangle ($\angle C = 90^\circ$) having integer side lengths. If its perimeter measures exactly $80\text{ cm}$, find the maximum possible area of $\triangle ABC$ in $\text{cm}^2$.
\end{enumerate}
